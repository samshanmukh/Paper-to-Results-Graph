Title: Lookahead Optimizer: k steps forward, 1 step back
Authors: Michael R. Zhang, James Lucas, Geoffrey Hinton, Jimmy Ba
Year: 2019
arXiv: 1907.08610

ABSTRACT
The vast majority of successful deep neural networks are trained using variants
of stochastic gradient descent (SGD). Recent attempts to improve SGD can be
broadly categorized into two approaches: (1) adaptive learning rate schemes,
such as AdaGrad and Adam, and (2) accelerated schemes, such as heavy-ball and
Nesterov momentum. We propose a new optimization algorithm, Lookahead, that is
orthogonal to these previous approaches and iteratively updates two sets of
weights. Intuitively, the algorithm chooses a search direction by looking ahead
at the sequence of "fast weights" generated by another optimizer. We show that
Lookahead improves the learning stability and lowers the variance of its inner
optimizer with negligible computation and memory cost. We empirically
demonstrate Lookahead can significantly improve the performance of SGD and
Adam, even with their default hyperparameter settings.

METHOD
Lookahead maintains slow weights phi and fast weights theta. For each outer
step, the fast weights are updated k times using any standard optimizer A
(the inner optimizer) starting from the current slow weights. The slow weights
are then moved toward the final fast weights:

    theta_{t,0} = phi_{t-1}
    theta_{t,i} = theta_{t,i-1} + A(L, theta_{t,i-1})    for i = 1..k
    phi_t = phi_{t-1} + alpha_slow * (theta_{t,k} - phi_{t-1})

Standard settings: k=5 inner steps, slow step size alpha_slow=0.5.

CLAIMS
1. Lookahead reduces the variance of the inner optimizer's iterates and
   improves learning stability across hyperparameter settings.
2. Lookahead-wrapped SGD and Adam achieve faster convergence (lower training
   loss at matched step budgets) than the raw inner optimizers with default
   hyperparameters.
3. The computational and memory overhead of Lookahead is negligible.

EXPERIMENTS
CIFAR-10/100 with ResNet-18, ImageNet with ResNet-50, LSTM language modeling
on Penn Treebank, and neural machine translation. Lookahead(SGD) and
Lookahead(Adam) match or outperform tuned baselines with lower variance
across seeds.
