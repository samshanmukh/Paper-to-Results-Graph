Title: Decoupled Weight Decay Regularization
Authors: Ilya Loshchilov, Frank Hutter
Year: 2017
arXiv: 1711.05101

[Excerpt — abstract and key sections condensed for hackathon demo]

ABSTRACT
L2 regularization and weight decay regularization are equivalent for standard
stochastic gradient descent (when rescaled by the learning rate), but as we
demonstrate this is not the case for adaptive gradient algorithms, such as
Adam. While common implementations of these algorithms employ L2
regularization (often calling it "weight decay" in what may be misleading due
to the inequivalence we expose), we propose a simple modification to recover
the original formulation of weight decay regularization by decoupling the
weight decay from the optimization steps taken w.r.t. the loss function. We
provide empirical evidence that our proposed modification (i) decouples the
optimal choice of weight decay factor from the setting of the learning rate
for both standard SGD and Adam and (ii) substantially improves Adam's
generalization performance, allowing it to compete with SGD with momentum on
image classification datasets.

METHOD (AdamW)
Standard Adam applies L2 regularization by adding lambda * theta to the
gradient before the adaptive moment computation. AdamW instead decouples the
weight decay, applying it directly to the parameters at update time:

    theta_t = theta_{t-1} - alpha * (m_hat / (sqrt(v_hat) + epsilon)
              + lambda * theta_{t-1})

This makes the effective regularization independent of the gradient magnitude
scaling performed by the adaptive method.

CLAIMS
1. L2 regularization and weight decay are NOT equivalent for Adam; standard
   Adam-with-L2 regularizes weights with large gradient magnitudes less than
   intended.
2. Decoupled weight decay (AdamW) substantially improves Adam's
   generalization, making it competitive with SGD with momentum on image
   classification (CIFAR-10, ImageNet32x32).
3. Decoupling makes the optimal weight decay factor independent of the
   learning rate, simplifying hyperparameter tuning.

EXPERIMENTS
CIFAR-10 and ImageNet32x32 with ResNet architectures: AdamW closes the
generalization gap between Adam and SGD-with-momentum reported in prior work.
